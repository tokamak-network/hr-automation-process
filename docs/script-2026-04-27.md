## Slide 1 — Title (10 sec)

Today I want to share some improvements we made to the candidate evaluation system.

## Slide 2 — First Candidate, First Lessons (60 sec)

We received our first real application through the system. Miirza Baig submitted a project called Tokamak Chain Monitor — a real-time monitoring dashboard for our L2 rollup chains. He also deployed a live demo on Vercel.

What's interesting is that he built it specifically for Tokamak — same stack, similar architecture to TRH. So it's clearly relevant to us.

But when we ran the evaluation, the system gave him an Ecosystem Fit score of 3 out of 10. That's wrong — this is literally a Tokamak-specific project. The reviewer matching also picked generic TypeScript developers instead of people who actually work on L2 infrastructure.

So this first real use case showed us exactly where the system needed improvement. Let me walk through what we changed.

---

## Slide 3 — AI Analysis (50 sec)

The main problem was that the AI prompt was too generic — just "How relevant is this to Ethereum L2?" It didn't know anything about Tokamak specifically.

Now the prompt includes our full technical context — Thanos Stack, OP Stack components, the TON seigniorage model, SeigManager, the challenge mechanism. Each scoring dimension has concrete criteria, so a score of 8 means something specific, not just the AI's feeling.

We also connected the Tokamak API running gpt-5.2-pro, so the analysis is real now.

---

## Slide 4 — New Criteria (50 sec)

We replaced two evaluation items that were hard to judge from code alone.

Innovation was too subjective. AI Proficiency — you can't tell from a repo if someone used Copilot.

Instead, we added Contribution Potential — can this work actually help Tokamak? And Deliverable Completeness — is there a working demo, not just code?

These are things you can clearly see from a repo and a deployed demo.

---

## Slide 5 — Reviewer Matching (50 sec)

The matching logic was only looking at programming languages. If someone used TypeScript, they got matched to TypeScript people, regardless of what they built.

Now it also looks at domain — rollup, bridge, staking, ZK, monitoring. And it reads the candidate's description and README, not just the code languages.

For Miirza, his description mentioned "tokamak chain monitor" and "trh ecosystem." That matched him to Theo, who works on tokamak-thanos and SentinAI — both in the rollup and monitoring domain. That's a much better match than just "TypeScript person."

---

## Slide 6 — Result & Next Steps (60 sec)

So here's the impact. Miirza's Ecosystem Fit went from 3 to 8. Contribution Potential scored 8 — new tooling that complements our repos. Deliverable Completeness scored 8 — there's a deployed demo. Overall, 4.33 became 6.83.

Based on these results, Theo scored highest in the reviewer matching. After this demo, I'll reach out to Theo through the HR recruiting channel and ask him to review Miirza's deliverable.
