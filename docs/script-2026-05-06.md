### Tokamak Network HR Solution — Weekly Update
### 2026.05.06 (~5 min)

## Slide 1 — Title (10 sec)

This week we have some minor upgrades to the existing features.

---

## Slide 2 — Overview (45 sec)

Most of the work this week was about improving what we already have — making workflows faster and fixing accuracy issues.

The biggest piece is the Payslip PDF, which got a major redesign. But before I go into that, I want to note — the current Payslip is specifically for Korea-based team members who receive their Service Fee in USDT. It doesn't yet cover non-KR-based members or Fiat salary recipients. That's something we plan to develop soon.

Other improvements include bulk status changes for payroll and expenses, and the ability to download candidate evaluation reports.

---

## Slide 3 — Bulk Status Changes (45 sec)

Previously, if you wanted to change the payroll status from "estimated" to "confirmed," you had to open each person's row, edit it, and save. With 8 people it's manageable, but as the team grows it becomes a problem.

Now there are three buttons — Estimated, Confirmed, Paid — that change the entire month at once. Same thing for expenses — Pending, Approved, Paid.

We also added a memo field to expenses. Since expenses are often paid together with the Service Fee, you can now note things like "Included in April Service Fee" without needing to enter a separate TX hash.

---

## Slide 4 — Unified Payroll + Expenses (50 sec)

This was a structural change. Before, the payroll table only showed the Service Fee amount. Expenses were in a completely separate tab.

Now the payroll table shows three columns side by side — Service Fee, Expenses, and Total. The expenses are automatically summed from the expense records for that member and month.

Important point — tax is only calculated on the Service Fee. Expenses are non-taxable. So if Ale receives 10,000 in Service Fee and 500 in expenses, the tax applies to 10,000 only, but the total transfer is 10,500.

The TX Hash field now supports multiple entries, since we often send to two wallets per person.

---

## Slide 5 — Payslip PDF (60 sec)

Again, this currently applies only to Korea-based USDT recipients. We'll build a separate format for Fiat and non-KR-based members.

The main layout change — expenses are now inside the Service Fee Details column, not at the bottom. The Subtotal shows first, then individual items below it, then the grand Total. This makes it easy to see the full picture at a glance.

For wallets, if a member has two addresses, they now show as "ERC20 Address 1" and "ERC20 Address 2" in separate rows. Same for transactions — "Transaction 1" and "Transaction 2."

On accuracy — we found that the Payslip was recalculating tax instead of using the stored value from the payroll table. This caused small differences, like 2,421 vs 2,430. Now it uses the exact value from the database. Also, the USDT conversion uses rounding instead of ceiling, matching what you see in the payroll table.

TX hashes are now clickable — they open the Etherscan page. And the PDF opens as a preview in the browser instead of downloading a file.

## Slide 6 — Candidate Report Download (30 sec)

Small but useful — when a candidate has been analyzed, you can now download the full report as a markdown file or PDF. This makes it easy to share with reviewers on Slack or keep offline records.
