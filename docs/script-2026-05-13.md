## Slide 2 — Background (50 sec)

I just finished the FY25 and FY26 draft accounting for the company. The biggest takeaway — classifying bank transactions took about 3 hours per fiscal year. Going through hundreds of transactions one by one, deciding what category each one belongs to.

Add exchange rate collection, invoice matching, balance sheet debugging — it was about a week of work total. And this repeats every year.

The good news is our system already has all the bank transaction data. So we added an accounting classification layer on top of it.

---

## Slide 3 — How It Works (45 sec)

The concept is simple. Every bank transaction has a counterparty name. We create a rule that maps each counterparty to an accounting category.

Once a rule is set, it applies forever. You click "Classify All," the system matches what it can, and anything new shows up for you to pick the right category. That choice becomes a new rule for next time.

We set up 23 accounts based on the actual FY26 financial statements — things like Sales, Salary, Subscription fee, Professional fee, and so on.

---

## Slide 4 — Result (50 sec)

We ran it on 799 bank transactions. First pass, 73% classified automatically. After adding rules for the remaining counterparties, we hit 100%. All 799 transactions classified with 97 rules.

Next fiscal year, maybe 10 new counterparties will show up. That's 5 minutes of work instead of 3 hours.

One extra feature — the system automatically flags payments to Korean residents for withholding tax review. It caught 31 transactions. We reviewed them and confirmed they're exempt under the Korea-Singapore tax treaty.
