## Slide 3 — Candidate Pipeline (50 sec)

First area — the candidate evaluation pipeline is now end-to-end.

Previously, we could submit a candidate and run the AI analysis, but that was it. Now there's a full workflow. After analysis, you can assign an internal reviewer, leave comments, and change the status to hired or rejected.

We also added evaluation compensation tracking. As you know, our process doesn't use resumes or traditional interviews. Candidates submit actual working code or a product as their evaluation. That takes real effort, so we compensate them for the deliverable — in TON, USDT, or ETH. The system now records the token type, amount, and the on-chain transaction hash.

This payment is for the deliverable itself, regardless of the hiring outcome. Think of it as our version of an interview fee.

We also fixed the AI report download so it works for every analyzed candidate, and the weighted score now shows one decimal place for better comparison.

---

## Slide 4 — Candidate Lifecycle Flow (20 sec)

Here's the full flow visually. A candidate submits their deliverable, AI analyzes it, an internal reviewer scores it, we make a hire or reject decision, and then we pay for the deliverable. Each step is logged — who did what, when.

The key point — the payment at the end compensates the candidate's effort in building the deliverable, regardless of whether they get hired. It's how we respect the work that goes into our evaluation process.

---

## Slide 5 — Invoice Module (50 sec)

Second area — we added an invoice management module under accounting.

The key feature is AI-powered PDF parsing. You upload an invoice PDF, the system extracts text from it, sends it to our AI, and automatically fills in the invoice number, counterparty name, total amount, currency, issue date, and due date.

This works with any invoice format. The AI adapts to different layouts, so it doesn't matter if it's from a law firm, a consulting company, or a cloud provider.

You can also upload multiple PDFs at once. The system processes them one by one and shows progress. There's also a manual entry form for cases where you don't have a PDF.

## Slide 7 — Document Repository (40 sec)

Third addition — an accounting document repository. This is where we store all the corporate filings.

Documents are organized by Assessment Year — YA2020 through YA2026 and beyond. There are three categories: financial statements like balance sheets and P&L, tax documents like Form C-S and NOA, and audit materials.

We have 6 years of existing documents to upload. Once they're in, anyone with access can find any filing instantly instead of searching through folders.

The year range automatically extends — next year YA2027 will appear without any code changes.
