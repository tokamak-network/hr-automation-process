## Slide 3 — Payroll: Bulk Selection & Delete (60 sec)

First — payroll now supports multi-select operations.

Previously, you could only delete payroll records one at a time. If you needed to clean up a month's worth of data — say, to re-import after a correction — you had to click delete six times, confirming each one.

Now there's a checkbox on every row, plus a "select all" checkbox in the header. When you select items, an action bar appears at the bottom showing how many are selected, with a bulk delete button.

This also sets us up for future bulk operations — like bulk export or bulk status changes for selected items, not just the entire month.

---

## Slide 4 — Payroll: Expense-Inclusive Recalculation (60 sec)

Second — we fixed how net pay is calculated when expenses are involved.

Previously, the net pay calculation only used the service fee. So if someone had a 1,950 USDT service fee plus 710 USDT in expenses, the net pay was calculated on 1,950 only. The expense showed up in the "total" column but wasn't reflected in the actual payout amount.

Now there's a "recalculate" button next to the bulk status buttons. When you click it, the system recalculates every record for that month — it pulls in the latest expense data, adds it to the service fee for the total, and recalculates net pay. Tax is still computed on the service fee only, since expenses are non-taxable reimbursements.

This is important because the net pay number is what goes into the payslip PDF and what we actually transfer.

## Slide 5 — USD Display in Totals (40 sec)

Third — we added USD amounts to the summary totals.

Both the payroll management page and the payroll calculation page now show dollar equivalents alongside the KRW totals. The tax total and net pay total in the footer row now display the USD conversion underneath.

This was a quick change, but it matters for us because we think in USDT but report in KRW. Having both visible at a glance saves the mental math.

## Slide 6 — Candidate: Inline Review Editing (30 sec)

One more small improvement on the candidate side. The reviewer and comment columns are now editable inline. You click the reviewer name or comment text, it turns into an input field, you edit and save — no modal, no page reload.

If there's no reviewer assigned yet, it shows a "+ Reviewer" placeholder you can click to start typing immediately.
